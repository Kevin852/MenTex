Journaling

Writing therapy is a form of cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and over time, can help alter negative thought patterns. Change the way you think, and you will change the way you feel, and change the way you act. Recent data is proving that Writing Therapy or Journaling is a beneficial therapy for those suffering from depression, anxiety, panic attacks and PTSD. . Who knew a daily diary could be so helpful?

Meditation

Meditation has been shown to reduce stress and increase levels of chemicals in the brain that are responsible for mood regulation and relaxation. Meditation helps clear the mind and eliminate unwanted emotions like anger, fear, resentment and sadness. When these emotions are not regulated properly they can result in neurotic behavior.

Behavioral Activation

Behavioral activation is one of the most important CBT skills used in treating depression. It has to do with the way that behaviors and feelings influence each other.Behavioral activation helps us understand how behaviors influence emotions, just like cognitive work helps us understand the connection between thoughts and emotions.

Pie Chart For Cognitive Behavioral Therapy

Cognitive therapy use of the pie chart is to, in graphic form, re-examine how you think about things. Writing our thoughts down can be a helpful tool to gain perspective, and creating a visual representation can be even more effective in impacting our unhelpful thought patterns.
